Meet Unofficial Project

This is my dance team!

Unofficial Project (UPro) is a collegiate hip-hop dance team housed at Boston University, established in the Spring of 2005. Its mission is to encourage diverse experiences and foster collaboration within the Boston dance community.

I joined UPro in Spring 2024 and fell in love with both the team and the greater Boston Dance Community! Each semester we engage in a range of enriching experiences that help us grow as dancers, teammates, and artists:

  • Beginning and End of Semester Workshops
  • Practices 3-5x a Week
  • 4-5 Competitions & Performances Each Semester
  • Team Bonding (Retreats, Family Dinners, Games, etc.)
  • Inter-collegiate Collaborations
  • Original Choreography, Formations, and Lighting
  • Photoshoots, Filming, and Promotions

Some Context on Hip Hop

Hip hop dance started in the 1970s in the Bronx, New York, as part of the city's street culture. It includes styles like breaking (breakdancing), locking, and popping—each with its own unique moves. Breaking features acrobatic floor work, locking is all about quick, sharp movements with pauses, and popping focuses on precise muscle contractions that create jerky, controlled motions. These styles grew out of the desire to express individuality, often in dance battles or community events. Today, hip hop is a major part of Boston's dance scene, especially in local colleges. The city's vibrant culture provides plenty of opportunities for dancers to learn, create, and perform. From dance crews to competitions and workshops, Boston offers a space where both beginners and pros can explore hip hop. It’s now a key influence in everything from competitive dance to fun performances.
